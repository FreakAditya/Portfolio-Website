Valorant Star yay Joins Bleed Esports for VCT Pacific 2024

Aditya Singh October 25, 2023 5 min read ⏰

Singapore-based organization Bleed Esports has announced the high-profile signing of Jaccob “yay” Whiteaker for their Valorant roster ahead of VCT Pacific 2024.


yay is considered one of the best Valorant players in the world, known for his incredible aim and clutch plays. He rose to prominence during his time with OpTic Gaming, helping them win Masters Reykjavik 2022 and placing 2nd at Valorant Champions 2021.

After leaving OpTic in October 2022, yay joined Cloud9 but struggled to find success. He then had a brief stint with Disguised in NA Challengers before becoming a free agent.

Now, yay will be moving from North America to compete in the Asia-Pacific region for the first time with Bleed Esports. He joins a talented roster including Deryeon, Egoist, crazyguy, and sScary.

“I wanted a new challenge competing in the APAC region,” said yay. “The unique Valorant style here intrigued me and I’m excited to develop my game with my new Bleed teammates.” yay said in a press release.

Bleed Esports had an excellent 2022 season, winning Ascension to qualify for VCT Pacific 2024. Adding a world-class player like yay raises expectations further for the Singapore squad. They will be a team to watch when the new VCT season begins.

With this high-profile signing, Bleed Esports cement themselves as a top contender in the Asia-Pacific region. All eyes will be on yay to see if he can regain MVP form and lead Bleed Esports to new heights next year.
