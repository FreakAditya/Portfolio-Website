Virat Kohli: India's Batting Maestro Continues to Shatter Records

Aditya Singh October 20, 2023 3 min read ⏰

Virat Kohli, the talismanic captain of the Indian cricket team, cemented his position as one of the greatest ODI batsmen of all time with yet another sublime century. On October 19, 2023, Kohli notched up his 48th ODI ton against Bangladesh in the ongoing ICC Cricket World Cup. With his latest century, Kohli broke numerous records and underlined why he is considered a modern-day legend.


Kohli's century was a masterclass in chasing under pressure. Walking in at no.3, he anchored India's chase of 257 with an artistic 97-ball 112 laced with 10 fours and 2 sixes. This was his sixth century of the 2023 World Cup, making him the first player to smash so many tons in a single edition. He also became the fastest to 48 ODI centuries, reaching the mark in just 234 innings.

Beyond records, what makes Kohli special is his insatiable hunger for runs. He is closing in on Sachin Tendulkar's 49 ODI tons and has a chance to become the first batsman ever to amass over 2000 runs in a World Cup edition. At 34, Kohli's appetite for success shows no signs of waning.

As captain too, Kohli has led by example. He is India's most successful Test skipper and has notched up an astounding seven double tons as captain. His brilliant double century against Bangladesh in 2017 proved his pedigree as a captain who revels under pressure.
